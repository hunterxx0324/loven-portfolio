- "our degrees / our schools"

## 5. Banned Rhetorical Formulas (Hard Rules)

1. **"In a World Where" Drama.** "In a world where [vague], [virtue] becomes [currency]." Banned: emotional setup without logical support.
2. **"Most People vs. The Few Who" Split.** Invents two groups, assigns behaviors without data.
3. **"Stop X. Start Y." Switch.** Creates false binary. Behavior change is not a light switch.
4. **"Not This. Not That. But This." Triple.** Feels nuanced. First two are usually strawmen.
5. **"If You're Not Doing X, You're Already Behind" FOMO.** Manufactures urgency via anxiety.
6. **"The Real Work Is..." Reveal.** Devalues concrete effort for invisible "mastery."
7. **"You Don't Need More X. You Need Y." Smack.** Abstract, non-actionable replacement.
8. **"It's Never Been Easier/Harder" Paradox.** Both halves unprovable superlatives.
9. **"Here's the Truth / Nobody Tells You" Fake Reveal.** Generic content dressed as hidden insight.

## 6. Extractability, Metadata, Structured Data

Same standards apply to: Citable response blocks, FAQ answers, metadata, schema text, JSON-LD fields, answer-first summaries, internal notes convertible to public copy.

**Required:**
- Use "housing allowance" not "BAH" (exceptions apply)
- Preserve platform identity. No provider-style phrasing.
- No benefit guarantees. No fake urgency. No inflated certainty.
- All banned words/formulas apply here. No bypass via schema or metadata.
- "Full BAH" as keyword target = intelligence only. Do not force into visible or structured copy.

## 7. Enforcement Checklist (Pre-Publish)

- [ ] Correct terminology used throughout
- [ ] Platform framed as guidance/verification tool, not school
- [ ] No banned words or phrases (except in official names, quotes, regulations, law)
- [ ] No banned rhetorical formulas or close variations
- [ ] No fake urgency, false contrast, inflated authority, unsupported certainty, invented specificity
- [ ] Schema, metadata, and Citable response blocks pass same standards
- [ ] Unavoidable official-source banned terms isolated. Not imitated in surrounding prose.
