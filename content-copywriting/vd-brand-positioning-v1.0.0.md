# VD Brand Positioning — Compressed
**Derivative of:** vd-brand-positioning-v1.0.0.md | **Compression target:** LLM context

---

## 1. Platform Identity [HARD RULE — zero compression]

VeteranDegrees.com = guidance and verification platform. NOT a school, university, college, degree-granting institution, enrollment office, benefits administrator, or VA decision-maker.

Function: helps veterans, service members, and eligible family members compare education options, understand program format, and verify details before enrollment.

---

## 2. Target Audience

**Primary:** Post-9/11 GI Bill users ages 22–40. Veteran parents managing transferred benefits. Dependents using transferred benefits.

**Common situations:** Newly separated. Transitioning active duty. Working while considering school. Veteran parent guiding a dependent. Dependent comparing education paths. Weighing income, family, schedule.

**Mindset (veterans):** How do I use benefits fully without wasting entitlement? Does format affect housing allowance? What must I verify before enrolling?

**Mindset (dependents/parents):** How do I avoid wasting transferred benefit months? What are the housing allowance implications? What requires verification before committing?

**Frame:** Benefits-wise decision-makers. Not system gamers. [HARD RULE]

---

## 3. Core Audience Needs (summary)

Understand benefits. Compare formats. Understand format-to-housing-allowance relationship. Identify what needs verification. Connect education to career, family, income. No condescension.

---

## 4. Platform Language [HARD RULE — zero compression]

**Use:** "programs listed on VeteranDegrees.com" / "schools found on VeteranDegrees.com" / "VeteranDegrees.com helps veterans compare..." / "helps users verify..."

**Never use:** "our degrees" / "our schools" / "our students" / "enroll through VeteranDegrees.com" / "apply to VeteranDegrees.com" / "VeteranDegrees.com pays/certifies/guarantees benefits"

---

## 5. Core Value Proposition

Helps users find, compare, and verify education options relative to career goals, family responsibilities, work schedule, and benefit strategy. Key focus: hybrid programs and how format may affect housing allowance.

---

## 6. Hybrid Program Framing [HARD RULE]

Always explain: hybrid = online coursework + in-person component. Format may affect monthly housing allowance because VA treats online-only and in-person attendance differently.

Always direct reader to verify: VA approval, program-level approval, modality certification, in-person component requirements, enrollment status, benefit chapter, and campus location.

---

## 7. Housing Allowance Terminology [HARD RULE]

**Use:** monthly housing allowance / full housing allowance / online-only housing allowance / housing allowance rate/calculation/outcome.

**Never use:** BAH (except unavoidable official context) / free money / guaranteed payment / housing hack / GI Bill loophole / "get paid to go to school" / any maximize-without-verification framing.

---

## 8. Benefit Strategy Framing [HARD RULE]

**Use:** "use earned benefits wisely" / "compare benefit outcomes before enrolling" / "protect your housing allowance by verifying" / "avoid wasting GI Bill entitlement."

**Never use:** "game the GI Bill" / "hack your benefits" / "exploit housing allowance rules" / "free money from the VA" / "guaranteed full payment."

---

## 9. Required Disclaimer Substance [HARD RULE]

Housing allowance outcomes depend on VA rules, school certification, enrollment status, course modality, benefit chapter, and location. Direct reader to SCO and official VA sources before enrollment.

---

## 10. Claims Requiring Source Verification [HARD RULE]

Never assert without current verification: exact rates, VA approval status, school participation, program modality, campus location rules, in-person requirements, benefit comparison amounts, Yellow Ribbon coverage.

---

## 11. Citable Response Block [HARD RULE]

Each major article needs ≥1 self-contained citable block. Must follow vd-ai-extractability-v1.0.0.md. Must preserve platform identity, benefit-safe framing, visible-content alignment, required caveats.

Must NOT imply VeteranDegrees.com offers programs, enrolls students, certifies/pays benefits, determines eligibility, or guarantees housing allowance outcomes.

Schema, FAQ answers, metadata, JSON-LD = same rules as visible copy. Structured data is not a loophole. [CROSS-DEPENDENCY: vd-ai-extractability-v1.0.0.md]
