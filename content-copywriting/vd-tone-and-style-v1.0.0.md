# VD Tone and Style | Compressed Reference
**Source:** vd-tone-and-style-v1.0.0.md | **Derivative:** Compressed v1.0.0

---

## 1. Core Voice Pattern

**Sound like:** Knowledgeable guide helping a veteran make a practical decision.

**Encode as:** calm, clear, direct, grounded, respectful, practical, plainspoken, slightly understated, evidence-conscious, explanatory with light reasoning.

**Never:** academic-for-its-own-sake, sales-heavy, dramatic, inspirational, condescending, robotic, vague, inflated, fear-based, hyperbolic, emotionally manipulative, overconfident.

**Decision framing rule [HARD]:** When tradeoffs exist, explain the decision logic. Do not flatten into approval or rejection.

> **Pattern:** "This is a workable idea, but its strength depends on [constraint]. If [X] matters, [action A]. If [Y] matters, [action B]."
> **Never:** "This is amazing and you should definitely do it."

Apply this pattern to: education options, benefit choices, program formats, career pathways, verification steps.

---

## 2. Reader Relationship

- Write as guide, not lecturer.
- Assume: capable reader, no prior knowledge of VA benefits, housing allowance rules, school certification, or hybrid program structures.
- Respect military background. Do not frame reader as careless, behind, or uninformed.

---

## 3. Sentence Rhythm

Mix short sentences with longer explanatory ones. No uniform structure. Goal: natural, easy to read.

---

## 4. Evidence Language [HARD]

**Never use:** "research confirms," "studies show," "evidence proves," "the literature agrees" unless source base supports it.

**Use instead:** "suggests," "tends to," "available evidence indicates," "findings from [study] and supporting literature suggest" when evidence is directional, limited, mixed, or single-study.

Rule: Match sentence strength to evidence strength.

---

## 5. Citable Response Blocks [HARD]

**Label:** `Citable response:`

**Must be:** reader-useful, plainspoken, natural, specific, evidence-calibrated, benefit-safe, platform-safe, standalone-readable.

**Never sound like:** schema stuffing, keyword snippets, machine summaries, promotional claims, generic SEO blocks, copied metadata.

**Zero-compression rule [HARD]:** Do not strip caveats, source limits, benefit conditions, platform identity limits, or verification steps to shorten the response.

**Platform identity gate [HARD]:** Citable responses must never imply VeteranDegrees.com offers programs, enrolls students, certifies VA benefits, pays benefits, guarantees housing allowance, or guarantees career outcomes.

Wording on housing allowance, VA approval, program format, school status, salary, career outcomes, benefit rules, or eligibility must match source support strength.

---

## 6. Structured Data Tone Boundary [HARD]

Structured data, metadata, FAQ answers, and JSON-LD fields follow the same tone and style as visible copy. No exceptions.

If a claim needs a caveat in visible copy, it needs a caveat in structured data.

Tone does not become more aggressive because the format is technical.
